## Player-Facing Truth

Players are told a simple truth:

- enter dangerous spaces
- collect book pages
- bring pages back
- spend pages in the camp shop to improve future runs

At this layer, pages are just the resource loop that funds survival.

## Hidden Truth Candidate

Book pages may not be ordinary pages.
They may be physical carriers of memory fragments or preserved human cognition.
The controlling authority values them for reasons far deeper than camp commerce.

## Why This Works

This creates a layered understanding:

- early game: pages are useful currency
- mid game: pages seem culturally strange and too important to be mundane loot
- late game: pages may be revealed as memory-bearing artifacts that the authority is harvesting

## Candidate Design Value

Using pages as the visible economy hides the deeper horror in plain sight.
Players keep participating in the collection loop before they fully understand
what they are actually helping recover.

## Open Questions

- Are pages literal page-like relics, or a stable visual form for memory matter?
- Does the camp shop knowingly process memory-bearing pages, or is it only an interface for the authority?
- Do some pages contain recognizable traces of prior slave teams or prior clones?
