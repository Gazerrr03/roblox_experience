- Type: run clue scene
- Placement: off-mainline, near the authored path from camp to maze
- Teaching target: first suspicion that the main maze predator hunts by sound

## Design Purpose

This clue is the first highly discoverable but still optional narrative-teaching scene in `Run`.
It should reward curiosity, seed dread, and prepare players for the first real sound-driven chase inside `Maze`.

## Hidden Background Truth

The corpse belonged to a previous slave-team survivor.
Their team entered `Maze` and was wiped out by the hearing-driven headline predator.
This survivor escaped alone, badly injured, and realized the creature tracked sound rather than sight.
They did not return to camp because failing the higher-dimensional authority's quota felt like a death sentence anyway.
They tried to survive in the wilderness and died there, abandoned.

This background truth is for design consistency, not direct exposition.

## Player-Visible Scene

The player should see:

- a body in a suit silhouette close to the current player shape
- the corpse has been dead for a long time
- the desert has partially reclaimed the body
- there are clear signs that the person tried to survive for a while before dying
- a dropped recorder or radio device remains near the body
- bags, scraps, or survival remnants suggest someone lasted longer than a clean instant kill

## Corpse State

The intended visual read is:

- not fresh
- not pristine
- partially swallowed by the wilderness
- visibly stranded
- visibly desperate

The player should read both of these at once:

- "something terrible happened before this"
- "this person still tried to hold on after escaping it"

## Interaction Structure

- the corpse scene is easy to notice from the main route
- the player must make a small voluntary detour to inspect it
- an investigation prompt triggers the clue
- the clue delivery medium is a radio or recorder fragment

## Audio Log Function

The audio log should communicate:

- panic
- shock
- fragmented field communication
- the sense that a whole team was erased quickly
- a final desperate realization that the threat is sound-driven, not sight-driven

The log should not explain the entire backstory.
It should only give the player enough material to suspect the rule.

## What The Player Should Infer

The player does not need to conclude the exact canonical truth.
It is enough if they come away with some version of:

- these dead people were like us
- they came from the maze or something close to it
- whatever killed them did not behave like a normal visual hunter
- noise may be what gets people killed
- escaping the maze is not the same as being safe

## What Should Remain Unsaid

Do not directly explain:

- clone replacement
- quota punishment
- the exact authority structure
- the full timeline of the survivor's final days
- whether this corpse was intentionally abandoned or simply never recovered

Those should remain available for later player inference.

## Maze Payoff

This clue is paid off when the player later meets the hearing-driven predator and instinctively sprints.
The encounter should make the earlier suspicion feel true in retrospect:

- noise draws the threat in
- panic worsens the situation
- stamina collapse turns the chase lethal

## UGC / Interpretation Value

This clue should be strong enough to spark player reconstruction.
Players should be able to argue about:

- who this person was
- why they never went back
- whether they were abandoned
- whether they were just an earlier version of us

The scene succeeds if it creates shared retelling, not just one correct reading.
