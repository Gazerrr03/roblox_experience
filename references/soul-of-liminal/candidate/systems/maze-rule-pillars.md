## Rule Pillar 1: Fragility

The player is easy to kill.
This is the emotional reason information matters.
Without fragility, clue discovery loses meaning.

## Rule Pillar 2: Time Pressure

The full `Run -> Maze -> Run` round is time-limited.
The player cannot solve everything safely and slowly.
The clock forces prioritization and risk.

## Rule Pillar 3: Uneven Enemy Rules

Not every enemy follows the same logic.
Current direction:

- one primary threat uses sound as its main rule
- two smaller threats are easier to resist and may rely more on sight or simpler logic

## Rule Pillar 4: Information Changes Play Indirectly

Clues do not need to grant explicit buffs.
The value of information is that players change their own decisions after learning it.

## Open Question

Should `Maze` have one dominant global rule, or a small hierarchy of rules?
Current candidate answer:

- sound is a major rule, but not the only one
- sight can govern at least one lighter enemy type
- traps do not need to obey the same detection logic as monsters
