## Candidate Clue Categories

- enemy behavior clues
- environment or mechanism clues
- world and mission clues
- hidden-item maps or stash pointers

## Delivery Form

The clue itself is primarily an interactable object.
Its surrounding scene dressing can include:

- bodies
- bags
- damaged equipment
- environmental traces
- props that imply what happened nearby

## Truth Model

Run should contain:

- true information
- false information
- ambiguous information that players must interpret

## Social Layer

Information is not automatically shared.
A player who discovers something in `Run` may only share it verbally.
This creates room for:

- honest reporting
- bad interpretation
- selective omission
- intentional deception

## Candidate Traitor Layer

A traitor role could know which clues are false while other players do not.
That makes `Run` exploration more valuable for the traitor because it gives them stronger material for manipulation.

## Design Risk

If misinformation is too strong too early, players may feel the game is arbitrary.
If misinformation is too weak, the social layer will not matter.
