## Current Loop Read

- Players begin in `Run`, framed as a ship camp and wilderness layer.
- Players explore `Run` to gather fragments, clues, and confidence.
- Players enter `Maze` because they are compelled to retrieve dangerous resources.
- Players face time pressure, lethal threats, traps, and uncertainty.
- Survivors attempt to bring resources back to `Run` before the round expires.
- Across rounds, players gradually understand the rules that govern survival.

## Candidate Story Framing

- Humanity is being manipulated by a higher-dimensional or cybernetic controlling force.
- Human workers are not honored explorers; they are managed, replaceable labor bodies.
- Death between rounds can be explained through clone-body replacement.
- Success earns better tools because a more effective worker saves replacement cost.

## Candidate Player Motivation

Surface motivation:

- retrieve required resources from the Maze
- collect book pages as the expedition's effective currency
- survive the round
- preserve tools and haul value back

Deeper motivation:

- understand why the system exists
- understand what is really being harvested
- realize that the loop itself is part of the horror

## Candidate Design Rule

`Run` should not exist just to route players into `Maze`.
It should shape the player's interpretation of danger before the player enters `Maze`.

## Candidate Player-Facing Framing

- Players are told to collect book pages.
- Players understand pages as practical value because pages buy tools and upgrades
  through the camp shop.
- Players do not initially understand what the pages truly are or why the
  controlling authority values them so highly.
