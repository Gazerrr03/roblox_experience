## Interval Theme

From mission-loop scaffolding toward the deeper soul of `Liminal`.

## Captured Fragments So Far

- `Run` should not be a disposable maze corridor.
- The ship, wilderness, and tower path should create curiosity before obligation.
- Players may be controlled humans or clone-like execution bodies under a higher-dimensional authority.
- The authority sends them into the Maze to retrieve dangerous resources.
- Players only understand those resources as book pages and camp currency at first.
- Rewards are operational: better tools for better workers.
- Death should cost carried resources and inventory, while revival remains fictionally grounded.
- Sound is emerging as a first-class rule for at least one major threat.
- The main predator is a fast, feral hearing-based hunter that investigates sound sources.
- The first chase fantasy is panic-running: sprinting buys distance, but noise plus stamina collapse leads to death.
- Voice communication may itself become dangerous if microphone sound later exposes teammates.
- `Run` clues may include true information, false information, and social deception.
- A traitor or saboteur layer is being considered as part of clue interpretation.
- Early clue teaching should come from corpse scenes and radio logs left by prior slave teams.
- Strong clue scenes should often live just off the authored main route as rewards for curiosity.
- The first corpse scene should depict a long-dead survivor partially reclaimed by the wilderness after failed attempts to live outside the maze.
- The player-facing entry motivation is mission-shaped: players begin already told they must go into the maze and retrieve pages.
- Current design reading: outer driver is mission pressure, inner growth is cognitive survival, final narrative meaning is tool-like exploitation.
- `Run` is now split more clearly into camp for preparation and wilderness for clue discovery.
- Long-term differentiation may involve social distrust and misinformation, but MVP should still stand on mission pressure plus information-driven survival.
- The team no longer wants to think in MVP terms; the active target is a deadline slice that already feels like a real game.
- Reuse should be selective and pragmatic: keep the parts of the current repo that accelerate the slice, ignore the rest.
- The shipping test is no longer `is there a prototype?`; it is `does one playable Run -> Maze -> Run loop already express the soul of Liminal?`
