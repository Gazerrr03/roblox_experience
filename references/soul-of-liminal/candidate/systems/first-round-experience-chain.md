## Run Opening Read

- `Run` is currently read as a desert exterior around the camp or ship.
- The route from camp to maze is authored in advance by design.
- Environmental dressing should gently bias players toward the intended path
  without turning the route into a hard corridor.

## Mainline Structure

- players leave the ship and see the desert and distant maze pull
- the authored route uses environment props and composition to suggest forward progress
- the player can stay on the mainline or drift outward into side exploration
- side exploration should sometimes pay off with stronger understanding than the main path provides

## Off-Mainline Reward Principle

Important clue scenes should often sit just off the main route rather than
directly on top of it.

This supports two goals:

- reward curiosity
- teach players that useful knowledge is not always sitting on the critical path

## Candidate First Teaching Chain

1. Player leaves camp and follows the desert route toward the maze.
2. A nearby side scene draws attention without fully stopping the route.
3. Curious players investigate a corpse site from a previous slave team.
4. They interact with a radio or recorder fragment.
5. The log conveys panic, pursuit, and a pattern that hints at sound-driven danger.
6. Players enter Maze carrying only suspicion, not certainty.
7. Inside Maze, the first major chase pays off the clue by punishing loud panic.

## Emotional Goal

The player should feel:

- the world is larger than the authored mainline
- curiosity can produce survivable advantage
- what happened to previous teams can happen to us
- the game teaches through eerie pattern recognition, not explicit tutorial language

## Candidate Traitor Interaction

If a traitor role exists later, off-mainline clue hunting becomes even more
valuable because the explorer controls what gets reported back to the group.
