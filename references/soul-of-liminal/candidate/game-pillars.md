## Reading Of The Latest Design Answers

The current answers suggest `Liminal` is not mainly:

- pure survival horror
- pure lore mystery
- pure extraction economy

It is closer to:

- a mission-driven horror expedition
- where survival depends on cognition
- under a system that treats the player as a tool

## Candidate Main Axis

Recommended current main axis:

`Liminal` is a mission-driven horror game where fragile players are forced into the Maze to complete assigned work, and only information-gathering and correct interpretation let them survive long enough to bring resources back.

## Why This Axis Fits

From the latest answers:

- the player enters `Maze` because the mission already demands it
- the player-facing motivation is operational and duty-shaped, not truth-seeking first
- the player's strongest real growth is cognitive, not stat-based
- the final narrative realization should be that the player was only ever a tool

This means the structure is:

- outer driver: mission pressure
- inner survival method: knowledge and interpretation
- ultimate narrative meaning: exploitation and expendability

## Candidate Three Pillars

### Pillar 1: Mission Pressure

The player does not casually choose to enter the Maze.
The game begins from obligation, assignment, and forward pressure.
The mission exists before the player's understanding does.

### Pillar 2: Cognitive Survival

The player is fragile.
Survival improves when the player gathers and interprets better information about monsters, environments, and risk.
Recognition and judgment matter more than raw stat escalation.

### Pillar 3: Exploited Existence

The deeper horror is not only the monster.
It is the realization that the player is an expendable worker or execution body inside a larger system.
The ending should cast the whole loop in that light.

## Run / Maze Role Split

Current best read:

- `Run/Camp`
  - preparation
  - economy
  - regrouping
  - assignment framing
- `Run/Wilderness`
  - information gathering
  - optional clue discovery
  - interpretation advantage before entry
- `Maze`
  - lethal execution layer
  - rule payoff layer
  - resource retrieval under pressure

## MVP Versus Long-Term Soul

Important distinction:

- for current MVP, the most important playable pillar is cognitive survival
- for long-term differentiation, social misinformation and distrust may become a major signature

So the project should not let future social systems erase the current base:

- mission pressure
- clue discovery
- rule learning
- fragile survival

## Candidate Recommendation Hook

Long-term recommendation hook candidate:

- \"The best part is how players have to judge information, suspicion, and each other under pressure.\"

But current MVP hook is more likely:

- \"You are fragile, under orders, and the only way to survive the Maze is to learn how its rules really work.\"

## Current Constraint

Do not over-center the social deception layer yet.
It is promising, but the core single-layer loop still needs to stand on its own.
