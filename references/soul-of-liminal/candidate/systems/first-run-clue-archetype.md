## Archetype

The first strong teaching clue in `Run` should be a corpse scene from a previous slave team.
The dead bodies should resemble the current players closely enough to create dread and implication.

This clue should sit just off the main route, not directly on top of it, so
investigation feels like a reward for curiosity rather than mandatory tutorialization.

## Scene Ingredients

- one or more bodies in protective suits similar to the player silhouette
- abandoned bags or dropped field gear
- clear signs of failed survival attempts after escaping the first massacre
- environmental traces suggesting sudden pursuit rather than a prolonged fight
- a nearby interactable investigation point
- an audio log or radio fragment recovered at the scene

## Intended Teaching Payload

This clue should not say the rule directly.
It should imply that the threat responds to sound, panic, or escalating noise.

## Example Emotional Read

- "They were just like us."
- "Something tracked them without needing a normal chase scene."
- "Whatever killed them cared about what they did, not just where they stood."

## Why Audio Logs Work

Audio logs let the player hear:

- breathing
- footsteps
- metal door sounds
- rising panic
- shock and fear in the voice performance
- abrupt interruption

This naturally supports the teaching goal for a sound-driven predator without turning into a tutorial pop-up.

## Open Questions

- Should the first clue be fully truthful, or partly distorted?
- Should the bodies be from a prior slave team, prior clone wave, or both?
- Should the recording be complete enough to imply the sound rule, or only enough to start suspicion?
